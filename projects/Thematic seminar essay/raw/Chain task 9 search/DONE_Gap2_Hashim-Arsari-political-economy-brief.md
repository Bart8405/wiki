# Gap 2 — Hashim Djojohadikusumo / Arsari Group: Focused Political-Economy Brief

*Synthesis note for Task 9 evidence-chase. This pulls together the Hashim/Arsari material already in the indonesia-carbon-market project and frames it for use in the Thematic Seminar Essay.*

---

## Why this matters for the essay

Framing 2 argues Indonesia is building a "credit-export economy, not a decarbonisation economy." The Hashim/Arsari network is the clearest single piece of evidence that this is not accidental policy drift — it is purposeful design. The person who designed the rules benefits from the rules. Understanding this network answers the essay's central question: *why* does Indonesia sequence ETS first and keep the carbon tax inactive?

---

## The core conflict of interest — one paragraph summary

**Hashim Djojohadikusumo** (Prabowo Subianto's younger brother) was appointed **Special Presidential Envoy for Climate and Energy** at COP29 in Baku, Azerbaijan (November 2024). He publicly claimed Indonesia had **557 million tonnes of CO2e verified and ready for trade** and announced a government target of **US$65 billion from carbon projects by 2028**. At the same COP29 pavilion, he attracted EUR 1.2 billion in green financing for Indonesia's electricity sector. He then recruited **ex-World Bank Managing Director Mari Elka Pangestu** to design the carbon trading mechanism for Indonesia. Meanwhile, a subsidiary of **Arsari Group** (his family conglomerate) — **ITCI Kartika Utama → ITCI Kayan Hutani** — is listed in government documents as a participant in the carbon trading rollout, with a forest concession in **Bulungan Regency, North Kalimantan**. The conflict of interest is structural: the rule-setter and the market participant are the same person's family.

## PR 110/2025 — the regulatory consequence

The regulation that Hashim's process produced contains a critical provision that directly serves the credit-export economy logic:

> **PR 110/2025 explicitly decouples voluntary carbon trading from the NDC timeline, allowing projects to generate and sell credits without waiting for national target fulfillment.** Corresponding Adjustments (CAs) — the mechanism that prevents double-counting by removing a credit from Indonesia's NDC accounting — are made **optional, not mandatory**, for international transfers.

**Translation for the essay:** under the new rules, Indonesia can export forest carbon credits internationally *without* those credits counting against Indonesia's own NDC commitment. This means the same tonne of avoided deforestation can simultaneously (a) be sold to a Japanese buyer who counts it toward their net-zero target, and (b) *not* be deducted from Indonesia's national emissions account. This is structurally a credit-export mechanism, not a domestic decarbonisation mechanism.

---

## The 13.4 billion tonne ambition — framing check

The Indonesian government has stated an ambition of selling **13.4 billion tonnes** of carbon credits to global buyers (timing unspecified; some sources say by 2060; others reference a 2028 horizon for early tranches). The Prabowo target is USD 65 billion from credits by 2028.

For context: Indonesia's total annual CO2 emissions are approximately 1.2 Gt/year. Selling 13.4 billion tonnes of credits implies either:
- Credits spanning several decades, OR
- Credits far exceeding any realistic domestic emissions reduction, relying on land-use accounting that critics (Pierucci et al. 2025; Rizzi 2025) say overstates genuine sequestration

The government presents this as a revenue opportunity. Critical readers (Walhi, Mongabay) present it as evidence that the system is designed to monetise existing forests rather than reduce emissions from the energy economy.

## Caveats for academic use

1. Tempo/Pulitzer reporting is investigative journalism, not peer-reviewed research. For an academic essay, use it as evidence for claims about actor networks, not as proof of illegality.
2. Hashim's conflict of interest is alleged by civil society (Walhi, Mongabay, Tempo); the government has not acknowledged it. The essay should frame this as "documented conflict of interest" not "confirmed corruption."
3. The Arsari Group/ITCI participation in the carbon market is confirmed by government documents reviewed by Tempo — not just an allegation.

Use Hashim/Arsari in **one focused paragraph** in the section that establishes Pattern D (credit-export economy). It is the named face of an otherwise abstract structural argument. The paragraph should:
1. Name Hashim and his dual role (envoy + beneficiary)
2. Name the regulatory consequence (PR 110/2025 decoupling)
