## Tier 1 — Core picks (the ones I'd actually add first)

### LITE (Lumentum) — strongest fundamentals + NVIDIA anchor

**Why now:**
- Q3 2026: record revenue **$808.4M**, beat consensus.
- Q4 guide: **$960M–$1.01B** revenue, op margin 35–36%, EPS $2.85–3.05. That's *acceleration*, not deceleration. ([Motley Fool transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/06/lumentum-lite-q3-2026-earnings-transcript/), [Sherwood News](https://sherwood.news/markets/lumentum-q3-2026-results-advanced-optics-networking-ai-boom/))
- NVIDIA **$2B preferred equity** + multi-year supply agreement. Cash position jumped to $3.17B. ([Simply Wall St](https://simplywall.st/stocks/us/tech/nasdaq-lite/lumentum-holdings/news/lumentum-holdings-lite-valuation-check-after-strong-q3-earni))
- Components revenue +77% YoY, Systems +121% YoY.

**Why now-ish, not now-now:**
- Already up ~180% YTD. Not a bargain.
- "Sell the news" hit it on May 6 earnings despite the beat.

**My read:** This is the cleanest "anchor a CPO position" name. Less speculative than AAOI, more pure-play optics than MRVL or AVGO.

### COHR (Coherent) — broadest portfolio + visibility into 2028

**Why now:**
- Q3 2026: revenue **$1.81B**, +21% YoY, beat consensus.
- **Bookings/backlog visibility extending into calendar 2028** — multi-year demand locked in. ([Yahoo](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/assessing-coherent-cohr-valuation-record-120653286.html))
- Management calls CPO a **$15B+ incremental TAM**. ([Stock Titan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/COHR/coherent-corp-reports-third-quarter-fiscal-2026-ffvom6za9vl3.html))
- NVIDIA **$2B equity stake** + multi-year CPO supply deal.
- Broadest optics product line in the group — less single-product risk than AAOI or CRDO.

**Why caution:**
- Forward PE ~43.6 (trailing ~89). Not cheap. ([Gurufocus](https://www.gurufocus.com/term/forward-pe-ratio/COHR))
- "Sell the news" on May 6, only +1% bottom-line beat.
- Larger cap = slower percentage moves up *and* down.

**My read:** If LITE is the "purest" anchor, COHR is the "safest" anchor. I'd own both rather than choose. They're not redundant — COHR is broader (lasers, materials, networking); LITE is more concentrated in datacom transceivers.

## Tier 2 — Defensive (if you want exposure without the beta)

### FN (Fabrinet) — the picks-and-shovels of picks-and-shovels

**Why:**
- Q3 2026: revenue **$1.214B**, +39% YoY, beat guidance. ([Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/04/fabrinet-fn-q3-2026-earnings-transcript/))
- Data Center Interconnect revenue **+90% YoY**, +38% sequentially. ([Tipranks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/fabrinet-earnings-call-highlights-surging-growth-tight-margins))
- Q4 guide: $1.25–$1.29B revenue.
- Contract manufacturer model = neutral to which CPO platform wins. Builds for everyone.
- Trades at lower multiple than the others.

**Why caution:**
- Gross margin only **12.1%** — that's how contract manufacturers work. Smaller earnings leverage to demand surprises.
- If demand softens, FN gets squeezed before the customers do.

**My read:** Lower-volatility way to play the theme. If you want exposure but lose sleep watching AAOI swing 10% a day, FN is the answer.

### MRVL (Marvell) — the mega-cap NVLink play

**Why:**
- **$2B NVIDIA preferred equity investment**, March 31 2026, tied to NVLink Fusion ecosystem. ([NVIDIA newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-ai-ecosystem-expands-as-marvell-joins-forces-through-nvlink-fusion), [Next Platform](https://www.nextplatform.com/connect/2026/03/31/the-2-billion-nvidia-deal-with-marvell-is-about-a-lot-more-than-nvlink-fusion/5213790))
- Partnership specifically includes "advanced optical interconnect solutions and silicon photonics technology."
- FY26 revenue $8.2B, data center **74%** of total.
- Analyst forecasts: ~$15B by FY28 (+40%).

**Why caution:**
- Big cap means the optics story is one of many drivers. You're getting diluted exposure.
- The XPU/ASIC business is more visible than the photonics piece.

**My read:** If you want a stock that benefits from CPO *and* survives if CPO timelines slip, MRVL has more legs. Less explosive upside.

## Tier 3 — Higher beta / event-driven

### CRDO (Credo) — June 1 earnings catalyst

**Why interesting:**
- FY2026 revenue guide **>$800M**, +85% YoY, targeting ~40% net margin. ([Tradingview/Zacks](https://www.tradingview.com/news/zacks:9f687e0be094b:0-is-credo-stock-a-buy-a-deep-dive-into-catalysts-risks-valuation/))
- AEC (copper) business is exposed to CPO disruption long-term, BUT Credo brought silicon photonics in-house — so they're hedged.
- Expected to add 2 new hyperscalers in 2H FY2026, going from 1-2 dominant customers to 3-4.
- **Q4 earnings June 1, 2026** — that's the next big catalyst.

**Why caution:**
- The AEC business *will* eventually shrink as CPO ramps. Bull case requires SiPho ramp to outpace AEC erosion.
- Goldman Sachs is bullish; consensus is becoming crowded.

**My read:** Don't buy *into* the June 1 print unless you're comfortable with a -15% gap risk. Wait for the print, then decide based on the SiPho guidance.

### AAOI (Applied Optoelectronics) — best YTD, biggest red flags

**Why bulls love it:**
- YTD +488%. Best in the group.
- **Booked out into mid-2027**, 10x capex expansion targeting $4.5B annualized revenue.

**Why I'd be cautious adding today:**
- **Customer concentration: 3 customers = 91% of 2025 revenue** (one at 39%, two at 31% and 21%). ([Seeking Alpha narrative](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4892529-applied-optoelectronics-has-a-vision-of-success-and-so-do-i))
- **Heavy insider selling**: $28.3M sold in the last 3 months, 12 transactions, **zero insider buying**. CFO and directors all selling. ([MarketBeat](https://www.marketbeat.com/stocks/NASDAQ/AAOI/insider-trades/))
- No NVIDIA equity anchor like LITE/COHR have.

**My read:** This is the highest-beta lottery ticket. The insider-selling pattern + concentration risk means I'd size it small (e.g. 1/3 the position size I'd put in LITE or COHR) or skip entirely. The fundamental ramp is real; the risk asymmetry is the issue.

## Tier 4 — Indirect exposure

### AVGO (Broadcom) — for those who want CPO inside a mega-cap

**Why:**
- Now shipping **Tomahawk 6 – Davisson**: world's first 102.4 Tbps Ethernet switch with 3rd-gen CPO. ([Broadcom](https://investors.broadcom.com/news-releases/news-release-details/broadcom-ships-tomahawk-6-worlds-first-1024-tbps-switch))
- 200G/lane CPO in production, 400G/lane on roadmap.
- ~$1T+ market cap, diversified, dividend.

**Why caution:**
- 2026 is "testing and qualification" year per management — high-volume CPO ramp is 2027+.
- Optics is a small slice of AVGO revenue; you won't get the pure-play upside.

**My read:** This is the "I want CPO exposure but I don't want to wake up to -15%" trade. Real but diluted.

## What I would actually do today

**If I had no exposure and a 3+ year horizon:**

1. Start with a **50/50 LITE + COHR** core position, sized to ~30-40% of intended total exposure today.
2. Add **20-30%** in either FN (defensive) or MRVL (mega-cap), depending on whether I want pure-play or diversification.
3. Keep **30-50%** in dry powder for further drawdowns (Iran/Hormuz could push another -15% over the next 1-2 weeks; NVDA earnings could surprise either way).
4. Optionally a **small** AAOI position (5-10% of the basket), eyes-open about the concentration risk.
5. Skip CRDO until after June 1 earnings. Skip POET entirely.

**If I already have exposure:**
- Don't average down into your largest position. Average down (if at all) into the *underweight* names in your basket.

**The mistake to avoid:**
- Going all-in on AAOI because it's "down the most." The names that fall hardest in a panic also have the highest fundamental risk *and* the biggest gains to give back.
