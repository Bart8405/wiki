Verra
Operates Verified Carbon Standard (VCS)▪
VCS is the world's most widely used greenhouse gas crediting program▪
Projects developed in this program undergo a rigorous assessment process -once 
certified, they are eligible to be issued Verified Carbon Units (VCUs)
▪
One VCU represents one metric tonne of carbon dioxide reduced or removed from the 
atmosphere
▪
How does it work?
A developer initiates the project, applies to Verra -an independent auditor and 
Verra experts visit the site and confirm the data, credits are then listed on the 
exchange, then buyers enter (they may have criteria -nature-based, no avoided 
deforestation controversies, high performance etc.

Big tech
The deals signed by Microsoft, Amazon, and Google in Indonesia are primarily "Infrastructure-plus-
Energy"deals. Because these companies are building massive data centers in Indonesia, they have 
signed agreements to power those facilities with 100% renewable energy—often through a mechanism 
called Corporate Renewable Energy Procurement.
As of early 2026, here are the specific deals that link these tech giants to the Indonesian grid and carbon 
market:
1. Microsoft: The "Cloud & Green Power" Deal
Microsoft’s presence in Indonesia is centered on its first Data Center Region, which is officially scaling 
up in 2026.
The Deal:Microsoft signed a long-term agreement with the state utility PLNto procure renewable 
energy for its data centers.
•
Specific Mechanism:They utilize Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs). By purchasing RECs from 
PLN’s geothermal and hydro plants (like the Lahendong and Asahan projects mentioned earlier), 
Microsoft effectively "claims" the green electrons to meet its 100% renewable goal.
•
Carbon Angle:In early 2026, Microsoft was reported to be scouting Biocharand ARR 
(Afforestation)projects in Indonesia to help meet its "Carbon Negative" by 2030 goal, following its 
record-breaking global removals purchases.
•
2. Amazon: The "Solar Utility" Deal
Amazon (AWS) is currently the largest corporate purchaser of renewable energy in Indonesia.
The Deal:Amazon secured an agreement to procure 210 Megawatts (MW)of renewable energy 
from several new solar and wind projects across Indonesia.
•
Specifics:This includes the first utility-scale solar farmsin Indonesia that were unlocked 
specifically by corporate demand.Amazon’s commitment provided the "bankability" for local 
developers to actually start construction.
•
Carbon Angle:Amazon is a key participant in the Symbiosis Coalition(alongside Google and 
Meta), which specifically targets Nature-Based Removals.They have shown direct interest in 
Indonesian Blue Carbon (Mangroves)as a way to offset the "Scope 3" emissions of their logistics 
and shipping network in Southeast Asia.
•
3. Google: The "Clean Energy & Biodiversity" Deal
Google has focused its Indonesian strategy on Decarbonizing the Gridand AI-driven conservation.
)•
Carbon Angle:In late 2025, Google announced an expansion of its AI-for-Biodiversitywork in 
Indonesia, partnering with local reforestation projects to use satellite data for carbon verification.
While their largest recent "removal" deal was in the Amazon (Mombak project), they are using the 
same high-integrity blueprint for potential 2026 deals in Indonesian peatlands.
•
Why these deals matter for IDXCarbon
These "Big Tech" deals are the primary reason the Indonesian Governmentaccelerated the 2026 re-
launch.
Direct Demand:These companies cannotexpand their data centers in Indonesia unless they can 
find green power and high-quality carbon credits locally.
1.
Price Floor:Because these companies are willing to pay a "Green Premium," they ensure that 
projects listed on IDXCarbon (like the 40 projects we discussed) are financially viable.
2.
Technical Standards:The strict "Due Diligence" requirements of Microsoft and Google have forced 
Indonesian developers to adopt Verraand Gold Standardlevels of transparency, elevating the 
entire market.

Analyzing the "Retirement Details" for anonymous entries reveals that these buyers aren't usually individuals, but rather major 
corporations and investment firms using intermediariesor retail offset platforms.
Here are the most common descriptions and patterns found in your files for anonymous buyers:
1. The "On Behalf Of" Middlemen (Most Common)
In both files, the most frequent description involves a third-party service retiring credits for their clients. This is the "hunch" that these 
are tech, finance, or architecture firms that don't want to manage a Verra account themselves.
CNaught Customers:This appears dozens of times in the Katingan file. CNaught is a platform that allows companies to bake 
carbon offsets into their products or operations automatically.
•
Greenplaces:Another common entry. Greenplaces is a sustainability platform for small-to-medium businesses.•
Stand For Trees:In the Rimba Rayafile, you see many "Order #" entries. Stand For Trees is a high-profile retail platform where 
individuals or small businesses can buy credits to save a specific number
•
of trees.•

The drastic changes you see in the data—
where a 
company might retire millions of tons one year and 
zero the next—are rarely random. They are the result 
of three major forces: 
Indonesian regulatory 
freezes
, 
corporate "target years,"
and 
market 
integrity scandals.
Here is the context behind the spikes and drops for 
the major players.
1. The Indonesian "Regulatory 
Freeze" (2021–Present)
The most important context for this specific dataset 
is the Indonesian government’s intervention. 
Around 2021, Indonesia began freezing the 
international transfer and issuance of carbon 
credits to build its own national registry (
SRN
).
The Impact:
This created a massive supply 
bottleneck. Projects like 
Rimba Raya
(Project 674) had a massive issuance of 14.5 
million credits in 2021, but almost nothing 
since.
•
The Resulting Numbers:
Companies that 
had already bought "stockpiles" of credits 
continued to retire them (like Shell in 2023), 
but those who were buying "spot" or yearly 
could no longer find new Indonesian credits 
to retire, leading to the sharp drop-offs for 
many European buyers in 2022 and 2023.
•
2. The "Carbon Neutral LNG" 
Model (Shell, PetroChina, Tokyo 
Gas)
For the energy giants, the numbers fluctuate 
based on 
shipment deliveries
rather than general 
corporate goals.
The 2023 Shell Spike:
Shell is a pioneer in 
"Carbon Neutral LNG." When a specific 
cargo of liquefied natural gas is sold as 
"carbon neutral," the emissions from the 
entire lifecycle of that ship are offset in one 
go.
•
Why the spike?
In 2023, Shell retired over 5 
million credits. This likely corresponds to a 
cluster of specific long-term LNG contracts in 
Asia where the buyer requested offsetting at 
the point of delivery.
•
3. Corporate Goal Milestones 
(Volkswagen & Delta)
Many companies set "2020" or "2021" as their first 
major climate milestone year.
Volkswagen (2021 Peak):
VW’s massive 
4.3M retirement in 2021 was directly linked to 
the launch of their 
ID.3 and ID.4 electric 
vehicles
. They marketed these cars as 
"carbon neutral at delivery," meaning they 
had to retire a massive volume of credits 
(mostly from the Katingan project) to cover 
the manufacturing footprint of every car sold 
that year.
•
Delta Air Lines (2021 Spike):
Delta made a 
$1 billion commitment to carbon neutrality in 
2020. Their 2.25M retirement in 2021 was a 
"catch-up" retirement to satisfy their public 
pledge for the 2020-2021 fiscal year.
•
4. The "Integrity Crisis" of 2023
In early 2023, investigative reports (by 
The 
Guardian
and 
Die Zeit
) and scientific studies 
claimed that many REDD+ projects (avoided 
deforestation)—
including Indonesian projects—were 
over-calculating their impact.
The Reaction:
This sent a shockwave 
through the market. High-profile brands like 
Allianz
, 
Kering (Gucci)
, and 
Zalando
are 
visible in your data as significantly slowing 
down or stopping their Indonesian 
retirements after 2022/2023. They likely 
pivoted to other types of credits (like 
"Removals" or "Reforestation") to avoid the 
reputational risk associated with "Avoided 
Deforestation" credits.
•
5. The Crypto "Carbon-on-
Chain" Boom & Bust
The 
Crypto / Blockchain
category shows a clear 
spike in 2021 (720k tons) followed by a crash.
The Driver:
This was the era of 
KlimaDAO
and 
Toucan Protocol
. They "swept" the 
market, buying up cheap older credits to 
"bridge" them onto the blockchain.
•
The Crash:
When the crypto market crashed 
in 2022, the liquidity for these "On-Chain 
Carbon" tokens evaporated, and the 
retirements for this sector plummeted back to 
nearly zero.
•

The Growing Need for Carbon Credits in Aviation
The aviation industry is responsible for a significant share of global greenhouse gas 
emissions.Carbon creditsare becoming more essential for airlines aiming to cut emissions. This is 
because alternative technologies, like sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), are still costly and not fully 
developed.
UnderCORSIA, airlines must offset emissions above 2019 levels. They do this by buying carbon 
credits from approved projects that reduce or remove greenhouse gases.
The demand for high-quality carbon credits will likely rise. This increase comes as more airlines 
and industries join compliance markets.ICAOrecently projected that 100-150 million tons of 
CORSIA Eligible Emissions Units (EEUs) will be required during the first compliance phase.

toactive
Reforestation (ARR)
or
Direct Air Capture

prove a new tree was planted or carbon was sucked out of the air than it is to 
prove a treewasn'tcut down.

What Is NOT in the Document
Notably absent —and this is standard for PDDs at the "Under Development" stage —are any

Red Flags Worth Investigating
The backstory described in the document is a classic avoided planned deforestation (APDD)
structure: PT. Agra Inti Investama held active oil palm plantation permits since 2007, decided 
in June 2022 to "shift" to forest conservation instead, and is now claiming carbon credits for 
not doing what they were licensed to do. This is one of the more controversial credit types in 
the voluntary carbon market, and critics argue it creates perverse incentives. The fact that 
Deloitte Hong Kong is structuring it adds to the sophistication.
