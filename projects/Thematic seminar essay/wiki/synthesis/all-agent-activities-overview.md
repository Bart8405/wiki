## For the essay specifically — what each file does

The essay has the most complex paper trail. Here is what each file is for:

### The original analysis files (written by the user + early agents)

**`wiki/synthesis/essay-direction.md`** — Written by Agent 1 on May 16. The working thesis, a full 5-section essay outline, a source map, and open questions. This is where the essay structure was first planned. Word target: ~3,700 words across 5 sections. Use this to understand the original scope.

**`wiki/synthesis/thesis-questions-answers.md`** — Written by Agent 3 on May 17. Answers directly: (1) why did Indonesia choose an ETS over a carbon tax, and (2) what are the five things Indonesia should do now. Clean, well-cited, and directly usable for essay sections 1 and 3. This is one of the most practically useful files.

**`wiki/synthesis/thesis-questions-answered.md`** — A parallel session's version of the same answers (also May 17). Similar content. Contains some additional source finds from the overnight search.

### The argument files (written by agents 8–9)

**`wiki/synthesis/argument-logic.md`** — The intellectual core of all the agent work on this essay. Contains:
- §1: Five cross-reading patterns (arguments that appear in 5+ independent sources — the strongest claims)
- §2: Three thesis framings, with Framing 2 recommended ("Indonesia is building a credit-export economy")
- §3: Counter-argument stress-test with responses
- §3a (added Task 9): What was found when searching for evidence against Framing 2 (short version: nothing strong was found)
- §4: Evidence gaps + sources to chase
- §5: Advisory essay skeleton (7 beats for Framing 2)
- §7 (added Task 9): The drafted thesis hook (~160 words, opening with Hashim at COP29) — ready to copy
- §8 (added Task 9): Chain task for this agent

**The three Gap files in `raw/Chain task 9 search/`** — Each is a focused research note:
- `DONE_Gap1...` — Indonesia's fossil-fuel subsidy system: IDR 713.5 trillion in 2024, 89% fossil-fuel, 8,000x bigger than the carbon market. The key number that makes Section 2's "hollow reality" argument.
- `DONE_Gap2...` — Hashim Djojohadikusumo and Arsari Group: the personal face of Framing 2's "credit-export economy" argument. The hook in the introduction is based on this.
- `DONE_Gap3...` — EU CBAM exposure for Indonesia: steel/aluminium/fertiliser exports face near-full EU carbon tariffs from January 2026. The fiscal argument for why Indonesia should raise its domestic price.

### The writing guide (written by this agent — Task 10)

**`wiki/synthesis/essay-writing-tomorrow.md`** — The file to open tomorrow morning. It consolidates everything above into a practical writing guide with:
- The thesis in one sentence
- Section-by-section word targets and paragraph plans
- A drafted introduction ready to copy and edit
- Sources listed by the section they belong to
- A fact-verification checklist

You do not need to open any other file to write the essay. Start there.
