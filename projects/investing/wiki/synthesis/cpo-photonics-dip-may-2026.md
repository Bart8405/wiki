# CPO & Photonics Dip — May 18, 2026

*Framework for thinking about today's selloff. This is NOT financial advice — it's a structured way to decide for yourself.*

## What happened today

The Nasdaq fell ~0.8% and AI-infrastructure names fell harder. Astera Labs (ALAB) dropped from ~$232.68 to $214.58 (roughly -7.8%). The broader optics group (AAOI, LITE, COHR, CRDO, FN) participated. Sources: [Sherwood News](https://sherwood.news/markets/speculative-stock-ai-quantum-computing-data-center-selloff-accelerates-iran-conflict/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/17/stock-market-today-live-updates.html).

## Why it happened (the cause matters more than the size)

**It is NOT about photonics fundamentals.** It is about three macro forces hitting at once:

1. **Middle East / Strait of Hormuz escalation.** Brent crude pushed near $110/bbl. ([Eastern Herald](https://easternherald.com/2026/05/16/wall-street-ai-stocks-crash-treasury-yields-spike/))
2. **Treasury yields spiked.** Higher rates compress the multiples of long-duration / high-growth stocks the hardest — and optics names trade at extreme multiples.
3. **Pre-NVIDIA-earnings caution.** NVDA reports later this week; the entire AI complex de-risks ahead. ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4594218-asia-markets-slide-as-middle-east-tensions-and-pre-nvidia-caution-weigh-on-tech))

So today is a *risk-off* day, not a *photonics-thesis-broken* day. That's an important distinction.

## The bull thesis (why people own these at all)

- NVIDIA Quantum-X (1H 2026) and Spectrum-X Photonics (2H 2026) switches are real shipping products with real capex behind them.
- CPO penetration projected to go from <1% to >35% of optical networking by 2030.
- NVIDIA put **$2B into Coherent and $2B into Lumentum** as anchor investments with multi-year purchase commitments — that's a hard demand signal.
- McKinsey: 800G transceiver supply will fall 40-60% short of demand through 2027.
- Applied Optoelectronics says it is booked out into mid-2027 and is doing a 10x capex expansion.

Sources: [NVIDIA newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-spectrum-x-co-packaged-optics-networking-switches-ai-factories), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/networking/nvidia-outlines-plans-for-using-light-for-communication-between-ai-gpus-by-2026-silicon-photonics-and-co-packaged-optics-may-become-mandatory-for-next-gen-ai-data-centers), [Genai Tech](https://www.genaitech.net/p/nvidias-4b-cpo-bet-scaleout-first).

## The bear case (what bulls hand-wave past)

- **YTD moves are extreme.** AAOI +488%, LITE +180%, COHR +112%. ([24/7 Wall St](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/12/which-optics-stock-has-dominated-in-2026-applied-optoelectronics-lumentum-or-coherent/))
- A -8% pullback from those levels is a rounding error, not a dip.
- "Sell the news" has been the recent pattern: record earnings, stock down anyway. ([TradingKey](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261872039-ai-optical-stocks-earnings-paradox-may-2026-tradingkey))
- Heavy **insider selling at ALAB** in early May (CEO sold $28M, COO $45.7M). ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/insider-trading-news/astera-labs-ceo-jitendra-mohan-sells-28m-in-stock-on-may-7-93CH-4678850))
- CPO has been "next year's tech" for ~5 years. Adoption could slip again.
- Single-customer risk: if NVIDIA throttles capex even modestly, the whole group de-rates.

## The "is this a dip" decision framework

Don't ask "is this a dip." Ask these four questions in order:

**1. What is your time horizon?**
- **< 6 months (trade):** A 7-8% pullback in a parabolic uptrend is usually not enough. Real swing-trade dips bottom 15-25% off highs. Wait.
- **2-5 years (investment):** Today's price relative to today is irrelevant. The question is whether the 2027-2030 thesis is intact. It is.

**2. What is your existing exposure?**
- **No position yet:** Starting full-size in a single name after a +488% YTD run is asymmetric risk. Consider a basket (LITE, COHR, FN) and a fractional starter position you can add to.
- **Already overweight:** Adding here would compound concentration risk. Most pros trim into strength, not add into weakness in their already-overweight name.

**3. Will you survive another -30%?**
- Photonics names have drawn down 30%+ three times this cycle (DeepSeek scare, March selloff, April tariff scare). If a further leg down would force you to sell, your size is too big *before* you add today.

**4. Why specifically these names vs. NVIDIA?**
- The "picks and shovels for AI" trade is partly already won. NVIDIA has more diverse cash flow, lower multiple, and is the buyer of the optics names. If you can't articulate why LITE outperforms NVDA from here, just own NVDA.

## What today actually changes about the thesis

Almost nothing. The bull case rests on **physics and capex**, not on this week's price action:

- Copper interconnect hits a power/distance wall above ~200 Gbps/lane. ✔ unchanged.
- AI clusters keep scaling out. ✔ unchanged.
- NVIDIA capex commitments to LITE/COHR. ✔ unchanged.
- Hyperscaler optics demand. ✔ unchanged.

The only thing today changes is the *price you pay* — and only marginally, since these are still near all-time highs.

## My honest read (still not advice)

A *macro-driven* dip in a *real* thesis is a better setup than a *fundamentals-driven* dip in a hype thesis. So this is a higher-quality buying opportunity than the average -8% day in a hot sector.

But: -8% from extreme YTD highs is not a "dip." It is normal volatility. If you'd been waiting for a 20-25% drawdown to start a position, you're not there yet. If you have a 3-year horizon and zero exposure, scaling in fractionally (1/3 today, 1/3 on a further -10%, 1/3 on -20%) is a more defensible entry than going full-size now.

The single highest-conviction trade in the basket is probably **LITE or COHR**, both anchored by NVIDIA's $2B commitments. AAOI is the highest-beta lottery ticket. FN is the safest (contract manufacturer, lower multiple).
