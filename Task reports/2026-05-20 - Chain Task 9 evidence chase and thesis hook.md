## What I did

### (a) Evidence gaps — three synthesis notes in `raw/Chain task 9 search/`

**Gap 1: IISD-GSI Indonesia fossil-fuel subsidies** (`DONE_Gap1_...`)

The headline finding: Indonesia spent **IDR 713.5 trillion (~USD 44 billion)** on energy subsidies in 2024, with 88.9% supporting fossil fuels. Total IDX Carbon trading since the market launched in September 2023: IDR 78 billion (USD 4.9 million). That ratio — roughly 8,000:1 — is the structural evidence for Objection 5's response. The essay can cite this directly as proof that Indonesia's "carbon price" is a fiscal illusion when the fossil fuel subsidy system dwarfs it by four orders of magnitude.

Key additional finding for the essay: the 3-kg LPG subsidy (IDR 80.2 trillion/year) is *regressive* — the richest 10% consume almost as much as the poorest 10%. This defuses the "household protection" argument for subsidy maintenance. The politically difficult component is industrial (PLN compensation, Pertamina, coal price cap) — the genuinely politically connected subsidy recipients are corporations, not the urban poor.

Source notes: IISD/GSI press release, IISD Energy Support Measures page, World Bank CCDR 2023 recommendation. Full IISD annual Indonesia report flagged for manual download.

Key findings:

- Indonesia's most exposed sectors to EU: steel (USD 726 million exported to EU in 2023), aluminium (USD 29 million), fertilisers (highest tariff rate at 138.72%)

### (b) Pressure-testing Framing 2 — counter-evidence search result

**Honest verdict: no strong counter-evidence found.**

The best candidates I searched:
- **BPDLH domestic abatement disbursements:** The mechanism exists. A 2024 UNDP/BPDLH program disbursed IDR 28.6 billion for domestic abatement. Scale: trivially small relative to the fossil fuel subsidy system. Does not challenge Framing 2.
- **PR 110/2025 centralised registry (SRUK):** Better institutional tracking. But the same regulation *weakens* domestic guardrails by decoupling VCM from NDC timeline and making Corresponding Adjustments optional. Net effect is weakening, not strengthening, domestic accountability.
- **JETP progress:** Only 14.5% of USD 21.4 billion committed has been approved. Coal retirement scaled back from 5.5 GW to 1.6 GW under consideration. Cirebon-1 retirement cancelled. This is the opposite of Framing 2 being challenged.
- **CBAM-driven price pressure:** The only genuine future pressure that could push Indonesia toward a higher domestic price for fiscal self-interest reasons. But this is prospective, not current.

I created a §3a "Genuine counter-evidence" section in argument-logic.md that reports these findings honestly, including the caveat that the BPDLH mechanism deserves acknowledgment. This strengthens the essay because it shows the author has engaged the evidence seriously rather than ignoring inconvenient material.
